Case Study · 5 Days · UI/UX Designer & Sprint Facilitator

Blu Vape Brand Reimagination

A 5-day Google Design Sprint that moved Blu Vape from brand ambiguity to a validated loyalty and community direction — with 87% user approval, in a single week.

Timeline
5 Days
Role
UX/UI Designer & Sprint Facilitator
Tools
Figma · Adobe XD · Miro · FigJam
Context
Regulated vaping industry

The Challenge

Blu Vape was losing market share to competitors who better understood customer expectations. The brand felt fragmented and product-focused, with limited loyalty or emotional connection. The sprint needed to deliver a validated direction — without months of speculation — while respecting regulatory constraints around age verification and responsible marketing.

Day 1

Understand

Facilitated alignment sessions with C-level stakeholders and department leads. Led customer journey mapping to identify friction, emotional drop-offs, and missed opportunities. By end of day the team aligned on a single challenge: how might Blu Vape build long-term relationships rather than one-off transactions?

Day 2

Diverge

Ran Crazy 8s and structured ideation — each team member worked independently before sharing. Themes emerged around customer loyalty, product trial, and tobacconist retail partnerships. Concepts were deliberately constrained to what could be prototyped and tested within 48 hours.

Day 3

Decide

Evaluated all concepts using dot voting and a decision matrix across desirability, business value, and feasibility. The winning concept — an integrated loyalty ecosystem connecting digital and physical retail — unified loyalty, trial, and tobacconist engagement into a single coherent direction.

Day 4

Prototype

Built mid-fidelity wireframes to validate information architecture, then a high-fidelity mobile prototype in Figma. Designed within regulatory requirements for the vaping industry — including age verification and responsible marketing constraints — while maintaining a strong brand feel.

Day 5

Test

Conducted moderated usability sessions with 8 participants — existing customers, potential new users, and tobacconist representatives. Testing confirmed the concept shifted brand perception from "selling products" to "building a relationship." Key usability issues around reward redemption and onboarding guidance were captured for iteration.

"Andrew's facilitation transformed our week. He navigated conflicting stakeholder priorities with diplomacy, kept us focused under tight deadlines, and delivered a prototype that exceeded expectations."

— Sarah Mitchell, Head of Product, Blu Vape
