The Challenge

Plant ownership is booming — but most plant parents struggle to keep their plants alive. Existing apps offered either overwhelming botanical databases or generic reminder tools that quickly became ignored. There was no product that felt personal, intelligent, and genuinely joyful to use.

"I've killed three plants this year because I just didn't know when to water them or what they actually needed. I didn't want a lecture — I just wanted something to guide me."
— Hannah, 27, urban plant parent

Key Problems

  • Generic reminders that don't adapt to plant type, pot size, season, or environment
  • No easy way to identify plants or diagnose problems with confidence
  • Information-heavy apps that overwhelm beginners and disengage experienced growers
  • No community layer — plant care felt isolated rather than shared
  • Lack of a single trusted source combining care, discovery, and community

Research: Understanding Plant Parents

I conducted 10 in-depth user interviews across beginner and experienced plant owners, supplemented by a 120-person survey, competitive app audit (PlantIn, Planta, Greg, PictureThis), and app store review analysis to surface unmet needs.

User Archetypes Identified

🌱
The Nervous Beginner
Keen but anxious. Kills plants regularly. Wants clear, jargon-free guidance and reassurance.
🪴
The Enthusiast
Has 10–30 plants. Wants smarter scheduling and a way to organise their growing collection.
🌳
The Community Sharer
Loves showing off progress, swapping cuttings, and connecting with fellow plant lovers.

Competitive Analysis Findings

  • PlantIn / PictureThis — Strong identification, but care advice is generic and not personalised
  • Planta — Good reminders, but expensive paywall and sterile UI
  • Greg — Smart scheduling, but community features are weak and onboarding is confusing
  • Gap: No app combines personalised care, beautiful UI, plant identification, and genuine community in a single cohesive experience

Key Research Insights

  • 67% of users had killed a plant in the last year due to improper watering
  • 82% said they'd trust a care reminder more if it adapted to their specific conditions (light, pot, season)
  • 71% wanted a way to share their plants and progress with others
  • Beginners needed emotional support as much as information — fear of failure was the biggest barrier
02

Insights: Defining the Design Challenge

Problem Statement: How might we design a plant care experience that feels personal, intelligent, and joyful — helping plant owners of all levels build confidence, form lasting care habits, and connect with a community that shares their passion?

Design Principles

🎯
Personal, not generic
Care guidance adapts to the specific plant, environment, and user skill level
😊
Joyful, not clinical
Plant care should feel like a hobby, not a chore — tone and visuals reinforce this
🔆
Clear, not overwhelming
Progressive disclosure — the right information at the right time
🌍
Community-powered
Plant care is better together — sharing, tips, and encouragement are core to the experience

Validate: Testing & Iteration

I conducted 3 rounds of usability testing across 15 participants, using Maze for unmoderated sessions and moderated video calls for deeper qualitative insight. Key iterations included simplifying the onboarding quiz from 8 questions to 3, redesigning the care dashboard after users missed the notification strip, and adding visual health indicators after testers found text-only status unclear.

Key Changes from Testing

  • Onboarding: Reduced from 8 screens to 4 — users abandoned at screen 5+ in first round
  • Care Dashboard: Moved watering tasks to top of home screen after eye-tracking showed users scanning upward
  • Plant Health: Added visual health meter (Excellent / Good / Needs Attention) after 7/10 users missed text-only status
  • Community: Added "Solved" tag to answered questions — reduced duplicate queries in beta by 34%

Reflection & Key Learnings

😊
Tone matters enormously
Users responded far more positively to encouraging, friendly microcopy than informational text
🔆
Progressive disclosure is everything
Showing too much information too soon overwhelms beginners — reveal at the right moment
🏆
Celebrate small wins
Streak rewards and milestone notifications drove the strongest daily engagement
🌍
Community is a retention engine
Users who posted at least once in the first week had 4x the 30-day retention of those who didn't
"This is the first plant app I've actually kept using past the first week. It feels like it was made for me — not for botanists."
— Beta tester, 31
