Blu Vape Brand Reimagination

A 5-Day Google Design Sprint — From ambiguity to a validated product and loyalty direction, all within one week.

The Challenge

Blu Vape was experiencing declining market share driven by increased competition, shifting customer expectations, and weakening brand loyalty.

"We're losing market share to competitors who better understand what customers want. Our brand feels outdated, and the experience is fragmented."
— Marketing Director, Blu Vape

Existing experiences were heavily product-focused, with limited emotional connection, long-term engagement, or community value. A 5-day design sprint was chosen to create alignment quickly, explore bold ideas safely, and validate direction before committing to further investment.

This project required careful navigation of the regulated vaping industry environment, balancing user experience goals with mandatory age verification, marketing restrictions, and consumer protection requirements.

Day 1: Understand — Mapping the Challenge

I facilitated strategic alignment sessions with C-level stakeholders, ensuring buy-in across marketing, product, and retail teams. Through structured stakeholder interviews and collaborative workshops, I helped the team articulate Blu Vape's vision, rebranding goals, and commercial challenges while managing competing priorities.

Key Activities

  • Stakeholder Interviews & Lightning Talks — Facilitated discussions to clarify business objectives and sprint success criteria
  • Competitive Analysis — Reviewed competitor approaches to loyalty, trial, and retail engagement
  • Customer Journey Mapping — Mapped the existing journey identifying friction points and emotional drop-offs
  • Synthesis & Problem Statements — Synthesised insights into clear objectives
How might Blu Vape build long-term relationships and brand loyalty, rather than one-off transactions?
— Sprint Challenge Statement, Day 1 Outcome

Day 2: Diverge — Exploring Wide-Ranging Solutions

We ran collaborative ideation sessions focused on loyalty, product trial, and tobacconist engagement. To avoid groupthink, each team member worked independently before sharing ideas.

  • Individual ideation followed by group critique
  • Thematic clustering to identify patterns and opportunities

Day 3: Decide — Converging on the Best Direction

All concepts were evaluated using dot voting and a decision matrix assessing desirability, business value, and technical feasibility.

Winning Concept: Integrated Loyalty Ecosystem

We selected an integrated loyalty ecosystem connecting digital experiences with physical retail touchpoints, emphasising community and personalisation.

Day 4: Prototype — Building to Learn

With limited time, my focus was on building just enough fidelity to test usability, value perception, and emotional response. I prioritised clarity, flow, and brand tone over exhaustive screen coverage — using Figma and Adobe XD for rapid prototyping.

Day 5: Test — Validating with Real Users

We conducted moderated usability testing with 8 participants, including existing customers, potential new users, and tobacconist representatives. Each session lasted approximately 45 minutes.
