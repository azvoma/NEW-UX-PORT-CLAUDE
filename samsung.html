Case Study · 3 Months · UI/UX Designer

Samsung B2B Lead Generation

Transformed Samsung's B2B digital presence from a product showcase into a consultative partnership platform — demonstrating industry expertise, building trust, and generating higher-quality leads in the Construction and PRS sectors.

Timeline
3 Months
Role
UX/UI Designer
Tools
Figma · Adobe XD · Miro · HTML/CSS/JS
Team
6-person cross-functional

The Challenge

Samsung's existing B2B presence was product-led — positioning them as a supplier rather than a strategic partner. Senior decision-makers in Construction and PRS responded poorly to generic feature messaging. The challenge was repositioning through design, not just copy.

01

Research

Facilitated cross-department workshops with Samsung's B2B marketing, sales, and technical teams. Conducted stakeholder interviews, qualitative research with target decision-makers, competitive analysis of competitor B2B pages, and a content strategy review. Key insight: senior stakeholders prioritise clarity, trust, and partnership credibility above technical specs.

02

Persona & Strategy

Designed around a mid-to-senior B2B decision-maker who is time-poor, outcome-focused, and skeptical of sales-led messaging. This persona shaped every structural and content decision — deprioritising product grids and promotional language in favour of scannable narratives, proof points, and consultative calls-to-action.

03

Ideation & IA

Explored multiple narrative and structural concepts through wireframing. Used a value vs. effort matrix to prioritise features. Deliberately avoided aggressive lead-capture forms and feature-heavy product sections — both of which reinforced the vendor perception. Designed a linear IA guiding users from industry context through partnership value to meaningful engagement.

04

Design & Prototype

Worked in an agile, multidisciplinary team alongside developers and marketing. Created low-fidelity wireframes to test narrative flow, then refined iteratively based on stakeholder feedback. High-fidelity designs met WCAG 2.2 AA — proper colour contrast, semantic HTML, keyboard navigation, and ARIA labels. Collaborated closely with engineering to ensure HTML/CSS/JS implementation was feasible.

"Andrew transformed how we present ourselves to B2B clients. He facilitated workshops across our marketing, sales, and technical teams, turning conflicting priorities into a unified vision. The new platform positions us as strategic partners, not just vendors."

— Michael Chen, B2B Marketing Director, Samsung Electronics

Outcomes
